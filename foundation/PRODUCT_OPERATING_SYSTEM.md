# BrytThrive — Product Operating System

> **Status:** Active
> **Last updated:** July 17, 2026

The Product Operating System defines how BrytThrive makes product decisions, prioritizes work, and evaluates whether a feature belongs in the product. It translates the [Constitution](./CONSTITUTION.md) into day-to-day operating practice.

---

## Core Operating Principle

Every feature must pass the Constitutional Test from [Article V](./CONSTITUTION.md#article-v--the-family-journey-principle):

1. Does this strengthen the parent's ability to create a healthy environment?
2. Does this increase the child's sense of ownership and autonomy?
3. Does this allow the AI to learn through authentic behavior rather than unnecessary data collection?

If the answer to any of these questions is no, the feature should be redesigned before it enters development.

---

## Permanent Product Tests

The following questions must be asked at every product review, sprint kickoff, and design critique. They are permanent — they do not expire or rotate.

### Are we onboarding motivation, or merely collecting data?

Every field, screen, and step added to onboarding must justify itself by improving the family's first experience — not by feeding internal analytics or deferring product thinking. Onboarding succeeds when a family achieves their first Family Win, not when an account is created.

*Derived from [Article V — Supporting Principle: Onboard Motivation, Not Data](./CONSTITUTION.md#supporting-principle-onboard-motivation-not-data)*

---

## Feature Decision Framework

When evaluating whether to build, defer, or decline a feature:

| Question | If No → |
|----------|---------|
| Does it serve a child or parent in an active session? | Defer |
| Does it pass all three Constitutional Tests? | Redesign |
| Does it make the first Family Win happen faster or more meaningfully? | Reconsider priority |
| Does it require data collection that could instead be learned through behavior? | Redesign |

---

## What BrytThrive Does Not Build

Features in the following categories are explicitly out of scope unless a future constitutional amendment provides a framework:

- AI-generated praise that replaces parent recognition
- Voice messages from the app to a child
- Push or email notifications designed to re-engage without parent intent
- Social mechanics that compare children to each other
- Reward economy redesigns that bypass the parent approval layer
- Full behavioral timelines not tied to a specific family goal

---

## The Family Win Definition

A Family Win is the atomic unit of BrytThrive's success metric.

A Family Win occurs when:

1. A child completes their first mission.
2. A parent notices and recognizes that effort.
3. The child feels seen, proud, and motivated to return tomorrow.

Product work that does not move families toward their first — or next — Family Win is not the highest priority.
